Building Strong Passwords

Below are some recommendations for creating strong passwords and general practices to keep your accounts secure.

Building Strong Passwords

  • Length Matters: Aim for passwords that are at least 12 to 16 characters long.
  • Mix Character Types: Combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
  • Use Passphrases: Consider a series of unrelated words or a memorable sentence.
  • Avoid Common Patterns: Steer clear of easily guessable words or sequences.
  • Uniqueness Is Key: Use a unique password for each account.
  • Consider a Password Manager: They can generate and store complex passwords securely.

What to Avoid

  • Personal Information: Avoid using data like your name or birthday.
  • Reusing Passwords: Don’t use the same password across multiple accounts.
  • Obvious Patterns: Avoid sequential numbers or common patterns.
  • Insecure Storage: Do not write down passwords or store them in unprotected files.

Additional Security Practices

  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Add an extra layer of verification.
  • Keep Software Updated: Updates often include security patches.
  • Beware of Phishing: Always verify emails or messages requesting login details.
  • Monitor Your Accounts: Set up alerts for suspicious activity.
