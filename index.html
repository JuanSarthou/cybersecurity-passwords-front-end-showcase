Building Strong Passwords

  • Length Matters:
    Aim for passwords that are at least 12 to 16 characters long.
  • Mix Character Types:
    Combine uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.
  • Use Passphrases:
    Consider using unrelated words or a memorable sentence.
  • Avoid Common Words and Patterns:
    Do not use easily guessable words or sequences.
  • Uniqueness Is Key:
    Use a unique password for each account.
  • Consider a Password Manager:
    A manager can generate and store complex passwords securely.

What to Avoid

  • Personal Information:
    Avoid using data like your name or birthday.
  • Reusing Passwords:
    Do not use the same password across multiple accounts.
  • Obvious Patterns or Sequences:
    Stay away from sequential numbers or common patterns.
  • Outdated Substitutions:
    Simple letter replacements (like "a" to "@") are not enough.
  • Storing Passwords Insecurely:
    Avoid writing passwords down or using unsecured files.

Additional Security Practices

  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):
    Add an extra verification step to secure your account.
  • Keep Software Updated:
    Updates often include essential security patches.
  • Be Cautious with Phishing Attempts:
    Verify emails and messages asking for login details.
  • Monitor Your Accounts:
    Set up alerts for suspicious activity.
